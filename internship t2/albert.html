Biography

Albert Einstein was a German-born physicist who developed the theory of relativity. He was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, and died on April 18, 1955, in Princeton, New Jersey, USA.

Throughout his life, Einstein made significant contributions to the field of physics, including the famous equation E=mcÂ². He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
